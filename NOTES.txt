wonder for a cumsum op over a 1D
tensor of u1*u2 shape (i.e. product of two original
reduction axis dim sizes), would it be a good idea
to have a "merge two reduction strategies" operation
 that merges the reduction strategies of u1 and u2
into a new 1D reduction? (or maybe should it just
create a new 1D reduction strategy specifically for
this cumsum?)

    codex
    Short answer: flatten first and treat it as a
    brand-new 1-D “scan” (prefix-sum) rather than
    trying to fuse two existing reduction states.

    Why

        1. ReductionStrategy in Helion is built
    around an associative, **commutative** op whose
    result is a single scalar per tile.  A
    prefix-scan has very different
    synchronization/order requirements, so you would
     need a dedicated “ScanStrategy” anyway.
        2. Collapsing the two logical axes into one
    physical axis early (during shape analysis) is
    easy: it’s the same trick we already use in
    `FlattenedTileStrategy`.  Once you have a single
     `(u1·u2)` dimension you can design the scan
    kernel without worrying about multi-axis
    bookkeeping.
        3. Trying to “merge” two existing
    ReductionStrategy objects becomes thorny:
           • Which of the two should dictate
    persistent vs. looped behaviour?
           • How do you combine their block-size
    specs when autotuning?
           • You still have to emit completely
    different code for a prefix-scan versus a plain
    reduction, so the shared logic is minimal.

    Recommended approach

    • When you see cumsum(x, dim=[d1, d2])
    (conceptually), reshape or view x to collapse d1
     and d2 into one dim d_flat.
    • Lower cumsum with a new ScanStrategy (or a
    specialised lowering pass) that only needs to
    handle a single reduction axis.
    • Re-use all the existing plumbing (block-size
    variables, masks, masking rules) just like we do
     for 1-D reductions today, but with
    scan-specific combine/propagate code.

    This keeps the compiler logic simple and avoids
    a bespoke “merge two reduction strategies” path
    that would only be used for scans.
